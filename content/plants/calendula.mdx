<span className="first">Y</span>es — calendula is safe for cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/aspca-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/pot-marigold) lists pot marigold (*Calendula officinalis*) as *"Non-Toxic to Dogs, Non-Toxic to Cats."* The plant and its edible petals pose no poisoning risk.

But there is a naming trap to clear up first, because it is the single most common source of confusion with this plant.

## Calendula is not the Tagetes "marigold"

Two unrelated garden plants are both called **"marigold,"** and only one of them is the ASPCA-listed safe plant:

- **Calendula / Pot Marigold — *Calendula officinalis* — SAFE (this page).** This is the plant the ASPCA lists as non-toxic. It is an edible, skin-soothing cottage-garden herb.
- **Tagetes Marigold — *Tagetes* spp. — NOT ASPCA-CLEARED.** The French and African "marigolds" sold in most seed packets belong to the genus *Tagetes*. They are **not on the ASPCA non-toxic list** and are widely reported to cause mild mouth, stomach, and skin irritation. Treat them as the one to avoid. See our [marigold page](/plants/marigold/) for the full Calendula-versus-Tagetes breakdown.

The lesson is the same as with so many common names: **read the Latin name.** *Calendula* is safe; *Tagetes* is not cleared.

## ASPCA Data

According to the ASPCA, pot marigold is listed under plants non-toxic to cats:

> Non-Toxic to Dogs, Non-Toxic to Cats.

(The ASPCA entry lists dogs and cats; it does not list horses either way.) There are no toxic principles for cats — calendula is completely safe.

## A gentle, edible herb

Calendula has a long history as a **culinary and skin-care herb** — the petals colour and garnish food and feature in soothing balms. For a cat, that means the plant is not just non-toxic but genuinely gentle: a nibbled petal or leaf is harmless. As always, a cat has no nutritional need for plant matter, and a large quantity of any greenery can cause mild, self-limiting stomach upset from the volume alone.

## Growing calendula around cats

Calendula is one of the easiest annuals: full sun, average free-draining soil, regular water, and deadheading to keep the flowers coming for months. It is happy in borders, pots, or a sunny windowsill, and makes a reliably cat-safe companion plant.

## The bottom line

Calendula — pot marigold, *Calendula officinalis* — is a safe, ASPCA-verified plant for homes and gardens with cats. Just keep it straight from the Tagetes "marigold," which is a different genus and not ASPCA-cleared. For other daisy-family plants and how they compare, see our [marigold](/plants/marigold/) page; note that both [chamomile](/plants/chamomile/) and [chrysanthemum](/plants/chrysanthemum/), despite the family resemblance, are *toxic* to cats.
