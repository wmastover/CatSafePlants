<span className="first">Y</span>es — the banana plant is safe for cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/aspca-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/banana) lists it as *"Non-Toxic to Dogs, Non-Toxic to Cats, Non-Toxic to Horses."* Both the plant and the fruit are non-toxic.

Banana (*Musa acuminata*) is a large tropical herb in the family Musaceae — despite its tree-like size, it is technically a giant herbaceous plant, not a tree. As a houseplant or garden specimen, its broad paddle-shaped leaves may tempt a curious cat, but they pose no poisoning risk.

## ASPCA Data

According to the ASPCA, banana is listed under plants non-toxic to cats:

> Non-Toxic to Dogs, Non-Toxic to Cats, Non-Toxic to Horses.

There are no toxic principles. The plant is completely safe around cats.

## Plantain disambiguation — two very different plants

The word **"plantain" causes real confusion**, so let's be precise:

- **Banana plant / cooking-banana "plantain"** — this is *Musa*, the same genus as the dessert banana. It is the plant the ASPCA lists as **non-toxic**. The starchy cooking banana sold as "plantain" comes from this group.
- **Lawn-weed "plantain"** — this is *Plantago* (for example *Plantago major*), a low broadleaf weed found in lawns and verges. It is an **entirely unrelated plant** in a different family.

When people ask "is plantain safe for cats?", the answer depends on which plant they mean. This page is about the **banana plant (*Musa*)**, which is safe. *Plantago* is a separate question and a separate plant.

## Feeding banana to a cat

Banana is a fine occasional treat but not a food:

- **Tiny portions.** Banana is high in sugar and starch — offer a small mashed or sliced piece, not a chunk.
- **No nutritional need.** Cats are **obligate carnivores**; they need meat protein, not fruit. A banana provides nothing essential their diet lacks.
- **Watch digestion.** Too much can cause loose stool or constipation from the sugar and starch, even though the plant is non-toxic.

## Growing a banana plant around cats

Banana plants want bright light, plenty of water in the growing season, rich free-draining soil, and warmth above 15°C. The dramatic leaves make a striking houseplant. Because the plant is non-toxic, there is no safety reason to fence it off — though you may want to discourage chewing simply to protect the foliage.

## The bottom line

The banana plant is safe for cats — leaves, stem, and fruit alike — and a banana is a harmless sugary treat in tiny amounts. Just keep the names straight: the safe plant is *Musa*, not the unrelated weed *Plantago*.
