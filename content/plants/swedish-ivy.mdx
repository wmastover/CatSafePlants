<span className="first">Y</span>es — Swedish ivy is safe for cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/swedish-ivy) lists *Plectranthus* (Swedish ivy / creeping Charlie) as non-toxic. Soft trailing stems fill hanging baskets and high shelves like [pothos](/plants/pothos) — without insoluble oxalate crystals.

Despite the name, this is not English ivy (*Hedera*), which is toxic. Swedish ivy is a South African Plectranthus with rounded leaves and purple-tinged stems.

### The pothos swap

[Pothos](/plants/pothos) is the default trailing houseplant — and one of the most chewed toxic plants in cat households. Oral pain and heavy drooling follow within minutes of chewing. Swedish ivy occupies the same macramé hanger safely.

### Pair with wax plant

[Wax plant](/plants/wax-plant) (Hoya) offers thicker, waxy trailing leaves on the adjacent shelf. [Spider plant](/plants/spider-plant) adds arching pups from a higher pot. All three are ASPCA non-toxic and replace [philodendron](/plants/philodendron) on the same wall.

### Pinch and propagate

Swedish ivy roots easily in water. If a cat damages trailing stems, cuttings recover quickly — a practical advantage over replacing a chewed pothos that was never safe to begin with.
