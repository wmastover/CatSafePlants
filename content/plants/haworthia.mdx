<span className="first">Y</span>es — haworthia is safe for cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/haworthia) lists *Haworthia* species as non-toxic. Striped rosette leaves fill the same sunny sill as [aloe vera](/plants/aloe-vera) without saponins or anthraquinone compounds.

Cats chew succulents for texture. Haworthia survives the investigation; aloe does not.

### The aloe swap

[Aloe vera](/plants/aloe-vera) is toxic to cats per ASPCA — vomiting, diarrhoea, and lethargy are common after ingestion. Haworthia offers the rosette silhouette buyers want on a kitchen windowsill. Read nursery labels carefully: the two are often displayed together.

### Not every succulent is safe

[Christmas cactus](/plants/christmas-cactus) is another ASPCA non-toxic option, with trailing segmented stems rather than a rosette. Jade, kalanchoe, and aloe are toxic. When in doubt, match the genus to ASPCA before purchase.

### Keep the pot stable

The plant is safe; a falling ceramic pot is not. Small haworthia rosettes suit elevated shelves or terrariums with ventilation if your cat treats windowsills as a buffet line.
