<span className="first">Y</span>es — philodendron is toxic to cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/philodendron) lists *Philodendron* species as toxic due to insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. Chewing any part causes immediate oral pain, drooling, and often vomiting — the same arum-family mechanism as [pothos](/plants/pothos) and [peace lily](/plants/peace-lily).

Heartleaf philodendron (*P. hederaceum*) is the most common indoor species, but the genus includes many vining and self-heading cultivars. Treat all philodendrons in the home as toxic unless you have verified a specific ASPCA entry stating otherwise.

### Not safe despite the rumours

Search results sometimes claim philodendron is "cat safe." The ASPCA classification is clear. Mild cases are common; mild is not the same as non-toxic. Repeated chewing increases exposure and stress for the cat.

### Monstera is a cousin

[Monstera](/plants/monstera) (*Monstera deliciosa*) is also an Araceae plant with oxalates. The ASPCA lists it separately as split-leaf philodendron. If you like split leaves, choose [calathea](/plants/calathea) for patterned foliage instead.

### Replacement plants

[Calathea](/plants/calathea) delivers tropical pattern without toxins. [Spider plant](/plants/spider-plant) and [Boston fern](/plants/boston-fern) suit the same bright-indirect corners philodendrons occupy.
