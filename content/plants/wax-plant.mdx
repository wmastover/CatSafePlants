<span className="first">Y</span>es — wax plant (Hoya) is safe for cats. The [ASPCA](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/wax-plant) lists *Hoya carnosa* as non-toxic. Thick waxy leaves trail from shelves and hanging pots like [pothos](/plants/pothos) — without raphides or oral pain.

[Pothos](/plants/pothos) and [philodendron](/plants/philodendron) are among the most chewed toxic vines in cat households. Hoya is the ASPCA-backed substitute for the same high shelf or macramé hanger.

### Trailing without oxalates

Insoluble calcium oxalate crystals in pothos cause immediate drooling and pawing at the mouth. Hoya's thick leaves store water and lack those crystals. Cats may still investigate — the outcome is uneventful.

### Blooms as a bonus

Mature Hoyas produce fragrant porcelain flowers — star-shaped clusters unlike lily blooms and without renal toxin. Keep stems elevated so buds survive curious batting.

### Pair with Swedish ivy

[Swedish ivy](/plants/swedish-ivy) offers softer trailing texture alongside Hoya's waxy leaves. Both are non-toxic and cover the spots where golden pothos once hung.
